# Security Policy

Threadway takes the security of our software and our users' data seriously. This
policy explains how to report vulnerabilities to us and what to expect in
response.

## Reporting a vulnerability

**Please do not file public GitHub issues for security reports.**

Send vulnerability reports to **[adam@threadway.ai](mailto:adam@threadway.ai)**.
If you'd like to encrypt the report, ask for a PGP key in your first message
and we'll send one back before you share details.

A useful report includes:

- A description of the issue and its impact
- Steps to reproduce (proof-of-concept code, request/response samples, or a
  short video are all welcome)
- The affected product, environment, and version (or commit SHA) if known
- Your name or handle for credit, or a note that you'd prefer to stay anonymous

## What to expect

- **Acknowledgement** within 3 business days of your report.
- **Initial assessment** within 10 business days, including a severity estimate
  and a rough remediation timeline.
- **Resolution updates** at least every 2 weeks until the issue is closed.
- **Public disclosure** coordinated with you — we'll agree on timing before
  publishing any advisory, and we'll credit you unless you ask us not to.

## Scope

Threadway is **pre-launch**. There is no generally-available product to test
against yet. Once we open early access, this policy will expand with a clear
list of in-scope assets and any safe-harbour terms for good-faith research.

In the meantime, if you've found something concerning in any Threadway-operated
property (including [threadway.ai](https://threadway.ai/) and any service it
references), we want to hear about it.

## Out of scope

- Reports generated solely by automated scanners with no validated impact
- Social-engineering or physical attacks against Threadway staff or facilities
- Denial-of-service testing against production endpoints
- Issues in third-party services we don't operate (please report those to the
  vendor directly)

## Bug bounty

We do not run a paid bug bounty programme at this time. We're grateful for
responsible disclosure and will credit reporters in any advisory we publish.
